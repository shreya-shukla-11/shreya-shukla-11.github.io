The Ideal Product

This is a story of failure. We had to generate the checkout page directly with carts given the menu of the restaurant, number of veg/non-veg people. There was a text box where a person could add their special preferences, maximize quality and diversity, apply the latest offer, adjust the cart to apply the most optimized offers/promos - all under 15 seconds and 2 Rs of cost per cart.
This problem statement is an obvious case for a multi-agentic framework of different types of models. Some models are good with Indian food context and what goes with what to ensure dish harmony, some are good with reasoning to optimize the cart for a certain number of people. But these traits you can only find in larger LLMs which have a certain cost and latency. After multiple tries with smaller LLMs and hundreds of agentic frameworks, we were not able to balance everything on this ideal product.
Good carts needed certain latency and cost, and if we compromised on it, then cart quality was not good. Eventually, we had to boil down the product requirements (learned it the hard way) 1 week before the launch.
